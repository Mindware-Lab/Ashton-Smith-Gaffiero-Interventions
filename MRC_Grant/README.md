# MRC Grant: Chronic Primary Pain Programme

This folder contains the working documents for a proposed **MRC Proof of Concept programme** to develop and evaluate a mechanism-led digital cognitive-affective intervention for chronic primary pain.

The pain intervention is the primary clinical product. It is supported by the **HRP Transfer Lab**, an enabling computational environment for mechanism modelling, measurement development, synthetic design validation, prospective candidate selection and study optimisation.

## Core development logic

```text
clinical problem
→ prespecified mechanism chain
→ mechanism-sensitive measurement
→ competing computational models
→ synthetic design validation
→ pain-specific human calibration
→ prospective intervention selection
→ protected and delayed transfer
→ functional outcomes and pathway fit
→ advance, revise or retire
```

## Key principles

* Human evidence remains the source of truth for clinical validity, safety, transfer and patient benefit.
* Synthetic data are used for design validation, model testing, power analysis and prospective prediction—not to inflate clinical evidence.
* AI has constrained, auditable and human-reviewed research roles.
* Mechanism mapping is product-directed rather than an open-ended investigation of chronic pain.
* Trained-task improvement is distinguished from mechanism change, protected transfer, functional transfer and delayed retention.
* The architecture is reusable but **domain-adaptive rather than domain-agnostic**: every future indication requires separate evidence and validation.
