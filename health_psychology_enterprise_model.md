
# Intervention-Intelligence Platform Model

| Enterprise capability                                               | Core question                                                                                                                                             | Specialised methods and explicit AI role                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             | Main outputs                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **1. Service-pathway, system and risk intelligence**                | Where are the bottlenecks, unmet needs, inequalities, risks, capacity constraints and failures in provision or delivery?                                  | Service and pathway mapping; organisational diagnostics; operations research; process mining; provision and inequality indices; geospatial and population analysis; risk and scenario modelling. AI can synthesise large document sets, administrative data and stakeholder evidence, identify patterns and model alternative pathways. Where consequential decisions are involved, human judgement, escalation, contestability and governance are designed into the system. Human–AI coordination is examined where AI forms part of the pathway, rather than being assumed in every project.                                       | Pathway maps, needs assessments, provision indices, risk maps, workflow diagnostics, service-gap analyses, system-redesign priorities and, where relevant, human–AI role maps and control-surface blueprints                                                                                                                         |
| **2. Intervention discovery and prioritisation**                    | Which mechanisms, services or intervention opportunities offer the greatest likely human and system value?                                                | Evidence synthesis; mechanism mapping; behavioural science; decision analysis; health or service economics. AI research agents search scientific, clinical, educational, organisational and intervention databases; identify candidate mechanisms and neglected opportunities; compare competing approaches; and support ranking by expected value, feasibility, equity, risk, implementation burden and evidence maturity. Human scientific and domain experts retain final prioritisation authority.                                                                                                                               | Evidence maps, opportunity portfolios, ranked intervention candidates, candidate intervention specifications, evidence-gap analyses, feasibility assessments and business cases                                                                                                                                                      |
| **3. Transfer-protocol engineering**                                | Which capacities, behaviours or policies can be changed, and do improvements generalise to meaningful functioning?                                        | **AI-Augmented Cloud Lab:** existing cognitive and intervention databases + AI research and design agents + computational cognitive models + synthetic participants and experiments + small, dense human pilots + prospective validation. AI accelerates protocol generation, comparison, stress-testing, optimisation and falsification. Human experimentation determines whether effects are genuine, safe, interpretable and transferable beyond the trained task or context.                                                                                                                                                     | Cognitive, affective and behavioural protocols; adaptive controllers; transfer ladders; wrapper and context-shift tests; functional-transfer measures; delayed re-checks; protocol manuals; and versioned, progressively validated protocols                                                                                         |
| **4. Measurement and psychometric engineering**                     | How can need, mechanism, change, risk and functional outcome be measured efficiently and defensibly?                                                      | Psychometrics; IRT and CAT; experimental measurement; longitudinal modelling; validation science; behavioural data analysis. AI supports item and task generation, synthetic response simulation, adaptive-test design, calibration experiments, error-signature discovery, dimensionality testing and scoring-model comparison. Constructs, items, scoring rules and consequential interpretations remain human-reviewed and prospectively validated.                                                                                                                                                                               | Scales, task-based assessments, adaptive tests, short forms, test batteries, item banks, capacity and risk models, scoring systems, confidence labels, measurement dashboards, norms and validation reports                                                                                                                          |
| **5. Digital experience, integration, access and adoption**         | How do users, practitioners and organisations enter, understand, use, integrate and sustain the intervention or service?                                  | Product and service design; UX research; accessibility; implementation science; behavioural engagement; systems integration; APIs; practitioner and manager tools. AI may support personalisation, conversational onboarding, accessibility adaptation, intervention routing, multilingual support, practitioner decision support and agentic control surfaces, within explicit protocol rules, confidence thresholds, escalation routes and governance constraints.                                                                                                                                                                 | Apps, websites, onboarding systems, APIs, practitioner tools, organisational control surfaces, adoption systems, implementation plans, integration specifications, engagement evidence and user-support resources                                                                                                                    |
| **6. Research, validation, publication and knowledge mobilisation** | Which findings should be independently tested, protected, published and translated into scientific, professional, policy and commercial use?              | Preregistration; reproducible research; protocol papers; psychometric validation; feasibility studies; controlled trials; prospective validation; implementation research; qualitative evaluation; health-economic and service-outcome analysis. AI may assist literature surveillance, analysis, coding, visualisation and manuscript preparation, with documented human oversight and authorship responsibility. Publication plans are reviewed for confidentiality, IP, participant consent, data protection and partner obligations before disclosure.                                                                           | Preregistrations, preprints, peer-reviewed papers, protocol papers, psychometric studies, feasibility and controlled trials, technical reports, white papers, conference outputs, implementation guides, policy briefs, evidence summaries, partner case studies and appropriately governed datasets or code releases                |
| **Cross-cutting governance, ethics, assurance and IP stewardship**  | What may be measured, inferred, recommended, automated, disclosed, owned, licensed or acted upon—and by whom, for what purpose and under what safeguards? | Research ethics; privacy and data rights; responsible AI; regulatory and clinical safety; safeguarding; equity and accessibility; decision-rights design; red-teaming; auditability; human escalation, appeal and override. IP stewardship includes knowledge-asset inventories, invention disclosure, background and foreground IP rules, contributor agreements, confidentiality, publication review, trade-secret controls, copyright, database rights, trade marks, patent assessment, licensing strategy and AI-output provenance. Governance applies whether a system is human-delivered, digitally delivered or AI-augmented. | Governance frameworks, ethics and regulatory approvals, assurance cases, risk registers, protocol and model cards, audit trails, data-rights controls, claims boundaries, decision-authority rules, IP registers, ownership records, publication-clearance decisions, licences, collaboration agreements and commercialisation plans |


## Overall structure

```text
1. Understand the system.
2. Find the highest-value intervention opportunities.
3. Engineer protocols designed to transfer.
4. Measure mechanisms and outcomes defensibly.
5. Deliver and integrate the intervention effectively.
6. Validate, publish and mobilise the knowledge.

Across all six:
govern ethically, protect rights, manage IP,
preserve human authority and control consequential use.
```
