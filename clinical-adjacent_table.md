
# Clinical-adjacent evidence map 

Current HRP Lab Stack Key:
AC = Attention Control
RM = Relational Memory / WMC
BM = Binding Memory
PP = Path Prediction
R = Reasoning / appraisal / inference
Z = Zone / readiness / state routing

| Condition / route                                            | Evidence signal                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          | Best IQM stack fit | Evidence strength for IQM-style category                                | Best public framing                                                        |
| ------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **ADHD**                                                     | Computerised cognitive training has been studied extensively in ADHD, especially attention, working memory and executive function. Recent evidence is mixed: some reviews report gains in attention/executive measures, while a 2025 evidence review found no clear effect on ADHD total symptoms or hyperactivity/impulsivity. ([PMC][1])                                               | AC, RM, BM, Z      | **Moderate for cognitive support; weaker for symptom treatment**        | “Attention and working-memory support for ADHD-style cognitive challenges” |
| **Traumatic brain injury / acquired brain injury**           | Cognitive rehabilitation for attention, memory and executive function after acquired brain injury/TBI has preliminary-to-moderate support; a 2025 meta-analysis of digital cognitive interventions reported significant executive-function improvement in TBI, though effect sizes are modest and protocols vary. ([PMC][2])                                                             | AC, RM, BM, PP, R  | **Moderate, especially with rehab partners**                            | “Cognitive rehabilitation support after brain injury”                      |
| **Stroke / post-stroke cognitive impairment**                | Digital and visual-skill/cognitive rehabilitation approaches have been studied post-stroke, with systematic reviews suggesting potential cognitive/executive benefits, though intervention types are heterogeneous. ([PMC][3])                                                                                                                                                           | AC, BM, PP, R      | **Moderate but heterogeneous**                                          | “Post-stroke cognitive rehabilitation support”                             |
| **Multiple sclerosis cognitive dysfunction**                 | A 2024 meta-analysis found cognitive rehabilitation can improve performance in MS, but authors noted that better methodology is still needed to identify which outcomes benefit most. ([PubMed][4])                                                                                                                                                                                      | AC, RM, BM, R      | **Moderate for cognitive rehab support**                                | “Cognitive performance support in MS”                                      |
| **Schizophrenia / psychosis cognitive remediation**          | This is one of the strongest clinical cognitive-remediation areas. Meta-analyses show small-to-moderate improvements in cognition and functioning, and a 2024 review found benefits can be durable. ([PMC][5])                                                                                                                                                                           | AC, RM, BM, R      | **Strong for supervised cognitive remediation; not consumer self-help** | “Cognitive remediation partner programme”                                  |
| **Major depression / residual cognitive impairment**         | Cognitive remediation in depression has meta-analytic support for improving global cognition, and newer work continues to support cognitive rehabilitation/remediation in MDD. WM and cognitive-control training are also studied for rumination. ([PMC][6])                                                                                                                             | AC, RM, R, Z       | **Moderate for cognition; emerging for mood/rumination outcomes**       | “Cognitive clarity and rumination-resilience support”                      |
| **Anxiety / stress resilience**                              | Working-memory training showed a small but significant reduction in post-training anxiety in a 2023 meta-analysis. Cognitive-bias modification has mixed evidence, but anxiety clearly overlaps with attention, WM load, threat capture and appraisal. ([PubMed][7])                                                                                                                     | AC, RM, PP, R, Z   | **Moderate for cognitive mechanisms; cautious for symptom claims**      | “Anxiety and stress-resilience skills”                                     |
| **PTSD / trauma-related attention control**                  | A 2024 review/meta-analysis found attention control training outperformed attentional bias modification for PTSD symptoms and attentional bias variability, though the evidence base is still relatively small. ([PMC][8])                                                                                                                                                               | AC, RM, Z, R       | **Emerging-to-moderate, specialist only**                               | “Attention control under trauma-related stress”                            |
| **Specific phobia / social anxiety exposure routes**         | The strongest digital evidence here is not classic cognitive training but **VR exposure therapy**. Recent reviews report strong evidence for VRET in specific phobias and promising evidence for social anxiety, especially when combined with CBT. ([ScienceDirect][9])                                                                                                                 | PP, R, Z           | **Strong for exposure-tech, weaker for IQM core stack alone**           | “Prediction and exposure-readiness support”                                |
| **Insomnia**                                                 | Digital CBT-I has strong evidence. A 2025 meta-analysis of 29 RCTs with 9,475 participants found fully automated digital CBT-I produced moderate-to-large improvements in insomnia severity, though therapist-assisted CBT-I was still superior. ([Nature][10])                                                                                                                          | Z, R, PP           | **Strong for CBT-I; indirect for IQM cognitive stack**                  | “Sleep-readiness and cognitive downshift support”                          |
| **Alcohol recovery / AUD**                                   | Strong mechanistic fit: WMC appears relevant to stress-related drinking. A 2026 EMA study in AUD reported that WMC moderated the stress–drinking association; people with lower WMC drank more in more stressful months, while higher-WMC participants did not show the same stress-linked increase. Cognitive training/remediation for SUD is also an active area. ([ResearchGate][11]) | RM, AC, PP, R, Z   | **Emerging but strategically interesting**                              | “Cognitive resilience for recovery”                                        |
| **Substance use disorders more broadly**                     | Reviews argue cognitive training/remediation could help manage cognitive deficits in SUD, especially inhibitory control, decision-making and executive function, but the field remains developing. ([Wiley Online Library][12])                                                                                                                                                          | AC, RM, PP, R      | **Emerging-to-moderate**                                                | “Recovery-support cognitive skills”                                        |
| **MCI / cognitive longevity**                                | Computerised cognitive training has meta-analytic support for memory/global cognition in MCI, but evidence is weaker for established dementia and long-term disease-modification claims remain unsupported. ([PMC][13])                                                                                                                                                                  | AC, RM, BM, PP, R  | **Moderate for MCI/cognitive longevity support**                        | “Cognitive longevity and memory support”                                   |
| **Dementia**                                                 | Cognitive training may help some specific functions or engagement, but effects are more limited than in MCI; it should not be framed as dementia treatment or prevention. ([PMC][13])                                                                                                                                                                                                    | AC, RM, BM         | **Weak-to-moderate supportive only**                                    | “Cognitive engagement for older adults”                                    |
| **OCD**                                                      | OCD has documented cognitive deficits and cognitive training has been attempted, but reviews describe the evidence base as small and underdeveloped. ([PMC][14])                                                                                                                                                                                                                         | AC, R, PP          | **Weak / exploratory**                                                  | “Cognitive flexibility and uncertainty-tolerance research route”           |
| **Autism / ASD executive-function support**                  | Executive-function training and exercise-based interventions have shown some benefits in children with ASD, including working memory, cognitive flexibility and inhibition, but generalisation and long-term effects are less clear. ([PMC][15])                                                                                                                                         | AC, RM, R, Z       | **Moderate for EF support; not autism treatment**                       | “Executive-function support for neurodivergent learners”                   |
| **Chronic pain / fibromyalgia / pain-related cognitive fog** | Chronic pain is associated with impairments in attention, memory and executive function; there is growing work on cognitive flexibility and CBT-based pain management, but direct IQM-style cognitive-training evidence is still emerging. ([ScienceDirect][16])                                                                                                                         | Z, AC, PP, R       | **Emerging / adjunctive**                                               | “Cognitive flexibility for pain self-management”                           |
| **Long COVID / ME-CFS brain fog**                            | Strong need, but evidence for cognitive retraining is still contested and sensitive. Best treated as research/rehab partner territory, not a public treatment claim.                                                                                                                                                                                                                     | AC, RM, Z          | **Weak / research-only for now**                                        | “Cognitive-fatigue support research route”                                 |
