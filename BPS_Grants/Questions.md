# Questions on the BPS Mathematics Anxiety Proposal

## Core theoretical framing

* I assume you agree with my interpretation that your dot-probe findings support a shift away from content-specific attentional-bias modification and towards a broader processing-efficiency / attentional-control intervention?

* Does the proposal fairly represent your 2026 findings: no reliable content-specific attentional bias, but slower responses across conditions in higher mathematics-anxiety participants?

* Would you be comfortable framing relative-frame CCC as targeting the kind of generalised control inefficiency your findings point towards?

* Is the hippocampal–prefrontal / relational-control angle useful, or should we keep the mechanism framed more conservatively around attentional control, working memory and context-dependent rule use?

## Intervention design

* Do you agree that the first BPS study should focus on the relative-frame CCC task only, rather than adding emotional working-memory and binding layers immediately?

* Would you prefer a three-arm parallel design with active control, absolute CCC and relative-frame CCC, or a counterbalanced sequential transfer design: absolute → relative versus relative → absolute, plus active control?

* Do you think the directional-transfer question is compelling: does relative-frame training transfer more strongly to absolute/mixed performance than absolute training transfers to relative-frame performance?

* If we use the counterbalanced transfer design, is 50 minutes of first-phase training likely to be enough to detect a useful directional-transfer signal, or would this be too weak?

* Would a design with more training before the first transfer probe be preferable, even if that increases participant burden? For example, should the first phase contain six or seven 10-minute sessions before the mid-test, rather than five?

* Would you prefer to keep the total dose at 10 sessions over 14 days, or should we consider a slightly longer protocol if the primary mechanism question is directional transfer? If the dose is too light for robust transfer, would you prefer the simpler three-arm parallel design, where each active group receives the full training dose in one task family?

* Should this BPS study be framed explicitly as estimating the minimum feasible training dose and early learning signal, rather than as testing a definitive training effect?




## Measures and outcomes

* Should MAS-UK be the main mathematics-anxiety measure, or would you recommend a shorter scale for screening and repeated testing?

* Should we include a broader test-anxiety measure as well, or keep the construct focused on mathematics anxiety?

* Which independent attention-control task would you prefer: Flanker, ANT, or something else?

* Should we include a simple reaction-time baseline to control for the generalised slowing effect?


## Emotional interference and future extension

* Do you agree that exam-threat words or evaluative distractors should be left out of the training in this first study, but possibly included as a transfer probe?

* For the follow-up grant, do you think emotional relational working memory and binding tasks are a strong next step? This would use an emotional n-back paradigm with exam-relevant emotional stimuli words (e.g. “deadline”, “fail”, “marked”, “mistake”).

* Would you want to pilot the emotional word set before using it in a full training task?

## Feasibility, recruitment and ethics

* Is King’s College London the right host institution for this proposal, and would it be feasible to administer the grant there?

* What participant source would be most realistic: KCL students, wider university students, Prolific, or a mixed recruitment strategy?

* Should we restrict the sample to moderate-to-high mathematics anxiety, or recruit a wider student sample and analyse mathematics anxiety continuously?

* Are there any ethical concerns around mild evaluative pressure in a maths-anxiety sample?

## Costing and administration

* Does the proposed participant-heavy budget seem realistic?

* * Would there be any KCL constraints/requirements? Would KCL require any overhead, indirect cost, or administrative charge, or can the full £6,000 be spent on direct research costs?

* Would a payment structure such as pre-test payment, per-session training payment, post-test payment, follow-up payment and completion bonus be acceptable?

* Is there a preferred route for participant payments, for example vouchers, bank transfer, research participation credits, or another system?

* Does the proposed recruitment target of around 90 participants, allowing for 20–25% attrition, seem feasible within the participant-payment budget?

* Ideas about existing student participant pools or course-credit systems that could reduce direct participant-payment costs?


## Analysis and larger-bid trajectory

* Should the primary mechanism outcome be relative-frame cost, directional transfer efficiency? Is the transfer metric of interest to you?

* Would mixed-effects models with time, arm and task type be the right main analysis approach?

* Should mathematics-anxiety change be explicitly exploratory rather than primary? Is the focus mechanistic or applied?

* What would make this pilot strong enough to support a larger grant application?

* Which future funder would be the best fit: ESRC, Nuffield, Wellcome, BPS, or an education-focused funder?

* Would you see the larger programme as primarily mathematics-anxiety research, cognitive-bias methodology, digital intervention development, or educational mental-health innovation?
